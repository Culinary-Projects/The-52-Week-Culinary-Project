    Weeks 53–54: Menus and Timing — Shopping List

    ← Weeks 51–52: Signature Dishes | Weeks 55–56: Final Exam →

    Equipment Check

    • [ ] Large roasting pan (for bigger cuts or multiple servings)
    • [ ] Extra half-sheet pans × 2 (for batch roasting)
    • [ ] Bain-marie setup (hotel pan + hot water; for sauce holding)
    • [ ] Serving platters (for family-style if applicable)
    • [ ] Champagne flutes or white wine glasses for first course
    • [ ] Extra large bowl for salad (scaled up)
    • [ ] Timer with multiple channels (multi-dish tracking)
    • [ ] Cocktail picks / small plates for canapés (W49 Fri)

    Week 49 Dinner Party #1 (4–6 guests)

    Shopping list is menu-dependent. The structure below assumes a French-influenced menu.
    Adjust items to match your chosen menu from the Week 49 Monday planning session.

    Assumed structure: 1. Canapés: Gravlax on blini (or smoked salmon), olives, cornichons 2. Starter: Individual salads or tartare 3. Main: Braise (short ribs, lamb shoulder, or chicken) + roasted vegetables 4. Dessert: Tiramisu or panna cotta (pre-made)

    Protein

    • [ ] Braising protein (choice): bone-in beef short ribs 4–6 lbs, OR lamb shoulder 3–4 lbs, OR whole chicken × 2
    • [ ] Smoked salmon or gravlax — 4–6 oz
    • [ ] Charcuterie (prosciutto, salami) — 4–6 oz mixed

    Produce

    • [ ] Shallots — 1 lb
    • [ ] Garlic — 2 heads
    • [ ] Carrots — 1 lb
    • [ ] Celery — 1 bunch
    • [ ] Root vegetables for roasting (parsnip, turnip, or potato) — 2 lbs
    • [ ] Fresh herbs (thyme, rosemary, parsley) — 1 bunch each
    • [ ] Salad greens (if starter is salad) — 2 heads

    Pantry

    • [ ] Wine for braising — 1 bottle (use restaurant-quality; it goes in the dish)
    • [ ] Beef or chicken stock — 1 quart (or use homemade from freezer)
    • [ ] Crusty bread — 2 loaves
    • [ ] Olives — 1 jar (canapés)
    • [ ] Cornichons — 1 jar

    Dessert (if tiramisu or panna cotta)

    • [ ] Mascarpone — 8 oz (if tiramisu)
    • [ ] Heavy cream — 1 pint
    • [ ] Ladyfingers — 1 package (if tiramisu)
    • [ ] Espresso — brewed strong day prior

    Week 50 Dinner Party #2 (6–10 guests)

    Scale your Week 49 menu by 1.5–2×, or design a new 5-course menu. This is intentionally flexible.

    Scale considerations (from quick-ref guide):

    • Braise: multiply protein weight 1.5–2×; liquid rises more slowly
    • Sauce: multiply 1.75× (less than recipe ratio for flavors)
    • Bread: one extra loaf per 4 guests
    • Butter for service: 1 stick per 6 guests

    Beverage planning (6–10 people)

    • [ ] White wine (aperitif / first course) — 3 bottles
    • [ ] Red wine (main course) — 4 bottles
    • [ ] Dessert wine or port — 1 bottle (small pours)
    • [ ] Sparkling water — 3 liters
    • [ ] Still water — 3 liters
    • [ ] Coffee / espresso for dessert — 1 lb whole bean

    Canapé station (pre-dinner nibbles for 10)

    • [ ] Charcuterie — 8–10 oz variety
    • [ ] Cheese selection — 2–3 types, 6–8 oz each
    • [ ] Crackers or quality bread — 2 packages
    • [ ] Seasonal fruit (grapes, figs, or dried fruit)
    • [ ] Nuts (Marcona almonds, walnuts)
    • [ ] Olives — 1 large jar

    Optional Wine Pairing (if planning pairings)

    See the wine pairing quick-ref. Bottle quantities for 8 guests: - Aperitif / sparkling: 2 bottles (standard 750ml; ~5 glasses/bottle, small pour) - White wine with starter: 2 bottles - Red wine with main: 3 bottles - Dessert wine: 1 bottle (2–3 oz pour per person)

    Pantry / Supply Top-Up

    • [ ] Extra virgin olive oil — full bottle (you burn through it at this volume)
    • [ ] Diamond Crystal kosher salt — full box
    • [ ] Maldon sea salt — for finishing plates
    • [ ] White balsamic or champagne vinegar — fresh
    • [ ] Dijon mustard — full jar
    • [ ] Fresh lemon — 1 bag (8–10 lemons for finishing and garnish)

    Planning Notes

    Week 49 dinner (4–6 guests): Keep menu modest and high-quality. Two or three excellent dishes are better than five stressful ones. Choose your most reliable braise — one that can be made 100% the day before. Run a practice timing session on Wednesday to find gaps.

    Week 50 dinner (6–10 guests): The main stressor will be logistics, not technique. The cooking is familiar. Plan for twice the mise en place time. Have a reserve dish in the refrigerator (charcuterie board, cheese, bread) in case any component requires more time than expected. Guests do not know the menu — they only know what they taste.

## The Make-Ahead Hierarchy

| Component | When to Prep | Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| Dessert | Day before | Anything that needs to set or chill overnight |
| Braise | Day before (or 2 days before) | Reheats better; sauce concentrates |
| Stocks and sauces | Day before | Refrigerate; reheat gently before service |
| Bread | Day before | Slice at service; wrap tightly |
| Starter (cold) | Morning of | Plate before guests arrive |
| Vegetables (prepped) | Morning of or afternoon | Covered in damp towel in refrigerator |
| Protein (final cook) | 20–30 min before service | The only truly à la minute element |
| Plating | During service | Pre-warm plates; garnishes staged |

## The Timeline-Building Process

**Start with your target service time (e.g., 7:00 PM) and work backward:**

| Time Before Service | What You're Doing |
|---|---|
| 3+ days before | Menu finalized; shopping list written |
| 2 days before | Long braises, stocks, desserts made |
| Day before | Bread, additional make-ahead components |
| Morning of | Vegetable prep; sauce base; mise en place |
| 2 hours before | Final prep; stress test the timeline |
| 1 hour before | Protein ready to cook; plates warming; table set |
| 30 min before | Table set; yourself ready; low-stress mode |
| Guests arrive | Final thing still cooking: 1 item maximum |

## Flavor Arc — Menu Composition Principles

| Position | Weight | Flavor Direction | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Amuse / nibbles** | Very light | Savory, snackable | Olives, charcuterie, blini |
| **Cold starter** | Light | Acid-forward, fresh | Salad, crudo, ceviche |
| **Soup (optional)** | Medium | Comfort, warmth | Bisque, consommé, velouté |
| **Main** | Heavy | Rich, satisfying | Braise, roast, pasta centerpiece |
| **Cheese (optional)** | Medium-heavy | Savory finish before sweet | 2–3 cheeses with accompaniments |
| **Dessert** | Light-sweet | Resolution, contrast | Panna cotta, tart, sorbets |

**Rule:** Never follow rich with rich. Every course should prep the palate for the next.

---

## Scaling — What Does and Doesn't Scale Linearly

| What Scales Linearly | What Does NOT Scale Linearly |
|---|---|
| Ingredient quantities | Cooking time for thick proteins |
| Basic ratios (1 tbsp per pound, etc.) | Oven temperatures |
| Seasoning by weight | Baking (chemistry changes at scale) |
| Braising liquid volume (roughly) | Reduction times |
| Sauce yields | Delicate emulsions (may break) |

**General rule:** Ingredients scale linearly. Time and heat do not.

## Wine & Beverage Pairing Quick Reference

| Dish Weight | Pairing Direction |
|---|---|
| Light (salads, seafood, delicate proteins) | Light-bodied, high-acid wine or sparkling |
| Rich (braises, cream sauces, roasts) | Full-bodied red or white; lower acid |
| Spicy | Off-dry Riesling, sparkling; avoid high tannin (amplifies heat) |
| Fatty/Rich (pork belly, duck, cheese) | High-acid cuts through fat; Champagne is the cheat code |
| Dessert | Wine must be sweeter than the dish; otherwise the wine tastes flat |

**For non-drinkers:** Treat shrubs (vinegar-fruit syrups), sparkling water with citrus, and herb-infused still water as seriously as wine pairings.

## Service Checklist

**Night before:**
- [ ] All make-ahead cooking done
- [ ] Mise en place list written for cooking day
- [ ] Table setting plan confirmed

**Day of:**
- [ ] Vegetable prep completed
- [ ] Starters plated and refrigerated
- [ ] Sauces made and warmed
- [ ] Plates warming in low oven or dishwasher safe warming setting
- [ ] Dessert ready or in refrigerator

**During service:**
- [ ] First course goes out before you sit down (or as guests sit)
- [ ] Clear each course completely before next appears
- [ ] Pour between courses; use the transition
- [ ] Last course cleared → dessert → coffee or tea → relax

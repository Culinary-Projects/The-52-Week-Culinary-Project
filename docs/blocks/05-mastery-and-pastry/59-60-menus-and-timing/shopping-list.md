# Block 59–60: Menus, Timing & Dinner Party Execution — Shopping List

*This block has no fixed recipes — you will be executing dinners from your own menu choices. The list below covers what you are likely to need for a dinner party of 4–6 and the planning materials.*

## This Block Is Different

Unlike every other block, Block 59–60 doesn't prescribe what you cook. You choose the menu from dishes you've learned throughout the program. The shopping list below is a **template and checklist**, not a fixed list.

**Template — adjust quantities to your menu and guest count:**

| Course | Ingredients | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Nibbles / amuse | Charcuterie, cheese, olives, bread, crackers | Put out immediately as guests arrive |
| Starter | Cold preparation of your choice | Already plated before guests sit |
| Main protein | 6 oz per person minimum (more for generous cuts) | Plan 10–20% extra |
| Main starch | 3–4 oz dry pasta or 6 oz potatoes per person | |
| Main vegetable | 4–6 oz per person | |
| Sauce | Enough to coat all plates (2–3 tbsp per person) | Make extra |
| Dessert | Fully pre-made | Account for dietary restrictions |
| Wine or beverage | 1 bottle per 2 guests minimum for a dinner party | Pair to menu |

## Session 10 — PROJECT: Full Dinner Party (6–10 Guests)

**Template — 5-course dinner:**

| Course | Category | Make-Ahead Status |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Canapés / nibbles | Purchased or simple from scratch | Same day or day before |
| 2. Cold starter | Salad, crudo, terrine, charcuterie | Morning of |
| 3. Optional soup or palate cleanser | Simple, elegant | Day before; reheat gently |
| 4. Main course + sides | Your centerpiece dish | Protein: day-of; sides: day before |
| 5. Dessert | Pre-made, unmolded at service | Day before |

**Quantities for 8 people (adjust):**

| Item | Quantity |
|---|---|
| Main protein | 6–8 oz per person = 3.5–4 lb total (bone-in adds weight) |
| Starter salad greens | 2 oz per person = 1 lb |
| Starch (dry pasta) | 3 oz per person = 1.5 lb dry |
| Two vegetable sides | 4 oz per person each = 2 lb each |
| Sauce | Double what you think you need |
| Wine | 1 bottle per 2–3 guests + 1 bottle reserve |
| Bread | 2 loaves for 8 |
| Butter | 1–2 sticks for the table |

## Notes for Planning

**Protein math:**
- 6 oz raw per person for a boneless centerpiece protein
- 8–10 oz raw per person for bone-in (bone adds weight; guests eat less net)
- Braises typically lose 25–30% weight during cooking — buy more than it seems you need

**Alcohol-free dinner party pairings to source:**
- Sparkling water (multiple bottles — this runs out)
- Citrus — lemon, lime for sparkling water or shrub drinks
- Shrub or drinking vinegar: look at specialty food stores or make your own
- Good-quality non-alcoholic sparkling wine if available

**For guests with dietary restrictions:**
- Ask at invitation time, not the day before
- Plan one substitution path for each restriction rather than an entirely separate meal
- Most vegetarian adaptations of your protein dish work well (roasted mushrooms, cauliflower steak, or a well-seasoned legume dish at the centerpiece)

# 🛒 Shopping List — Block 51–52: Capstone

← [Back to Full Guide](index.md) | [Back to Quick Ref](quick-ref.md)

---

> **There is no prescribed shopping list for the capstone.** You decide what to cook. The list below covers likely needs for the key sessions, but your Anniversary Dinner and Final Exam Dish menus are entirely yours.

---

## General Pantry Restock

By Block 51, your pantry should be well-stocked from the year's cooking. Check and restock as needed:

- [ ] **Kosher salt** — Diamond Crystal
- [ ] **Black pepper** — whole peppercorns
- [ ] **Olive oil** — good for finishing
- [ ] **Neutral oil** — for high-heat cooking
- [ ] **Butter** — unsalted, European-style
- [ ] **Eggs** — 1 dozen
- [ ] **Onions, garlic, shallots** — the holy trinity of your mise en place
- [ ] **Lemons** — always useful
- [ ] **Stock** — homemade from the freezer, or make a fresh batch

---

## For the Anniversary Dinner (Service 56)

Plan your own menu. Consider:

- [ ] A protein you're confident with
- [ ] A starch or bread you can make in your sleep
- [ ] A vegetable preparation that shows technique
- [ ] A dessert from Blocks 47–50
- [ ] Wine, sparkling water, or cocktails for guests
- [ ] Table settings, candles, music — set the scene

---

## For the Final Exam Dish (Session 230)

- [ ] Whatever your chosen dish requires
- [ ] Buy the best version of each ingredient — this is your showcase
- [ ] Have backup ingredients in case something goes wrong

---

## For the Retrospective Meal (Session 231)

You'll need ingredients for 4 small courses spanning the program:

- [ ] One element from Blocks 1–10 (foundations): check your notes
- [ ] One element from Blocks 11–24 (proteins): your best protein prep
- [ ] One element from Blocks 25–40 (world cuisines): the cuisine that resonated most
- [ ] One element from Blocks 41–50 (fermentation, bread, pastry): your proudest bake or ferment

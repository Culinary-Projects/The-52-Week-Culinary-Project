# 🛒 Shopping List — Block 39–44: Bread

← [Back to Full Guide](index.md) | [Back to Quick Ref](quick-ref.md)

---

> **Bread shopping is mostly flour.** Get good flour — it matters here more than anywhere else in this program. King Arthur Bread Flour is reliable and widely available. For sourdough and higher-hydration loaves, bread flour (higher protein) performs better than all-purpose.

---

## ⚠️ Equipment Check

- [ ] **Dutch oven** — essential for no-knead and sourdough; 5–7 quart, cast iron
- [ ] **Pullman pan with lid** — for sandwich bread (Block 41); ~$25–35
- [ ] **Banneton (proofing basket)** — for sourdough shaping (Block 42); optional but helpful
- [ ] **Bench scraper** — for dough handling
- [ ] **Kitchen scale** — bread is by grams, not cups; this is mandatory here
- [ ] **Lame** — a specialized razor blade for scoring sourdough; a very sharp knife works
- [ ] **Stand mixer with dough hook** — for brioche (the butter incorporation is nearly impossible by hand)
- [ ] **Instant-read thermometer** — internal bread temp: 200–210°F when fully baked

---

## 🌾 Flour (buy more than you think you need)

- [ ] **Bread flour** — 10 lbs (no-knead, pullman, sourdough, croissants)
- [ ] **All-purpose flour** — 2 lbs (starter feeding, some focaccia applications)
- [ ] **Whole wheat flour** — 1 lb (starter feeding; some add to sourdough for flavor)
- [ ] **Rye flour** — small bag (sourdough starter boost; optional)

---

## 🧂 Pantry

- [ ] **Kosher salt** — Diamond Crystal; other salts change hydration ratios
- [ ] **Active dry yeast or instant yeast** — 1–2 packets (no-knead, pullman, brioche, croissants; sourdough uses starter)
- [ ] **Olive oil** — 1 large bottle (focaccia uses a substantial quantity)
- [ ] **Butter, European-style** — 2 lbs (brioche and croissants are butter-hungry; European-style has less water)
- [ ] **Whole milk** — 1 quart (pullman enrichment, brioche)
- [ ] **Heavy cream** — ½ pint (enriched doughs + egg wash for croissants)
- [ ] **Eggs** — 1 dozen (brioche dough + egg washes)
- [ ] **Sugar / honey** — small amounts (enriched doughs)

---

## 🍞 Toppings and Finishing

- [ ] **Fleur de sel or Maldon** — for focaccia topping
- [ ] **Fresh rosemary** — 2 sprigs (focaccia)
- [ ] **Cherry tomatoes** — for focaccia topping (Block 41)
- [ ] **Olives** — 1 small jar (focaccia option)
- [ ] **Cultured butter** — Kerrygold or similar (for the bread tastings — this is the fun part)
- [ ] **Good olive oil** — for dipping alongside fresh bread
- [ ] **Jam** — 2 varieties (for brioche)
- [ ] **Pain au chocolat chocolate sticks or bittersweet chocolate** — for chocolate croissants (Block 42)

---

## For Cheese and Charcuterie Board (Block 42)

- [ ] 2–3 cheeses (firm and soft)
- [ ] Prosciutto or salami
- [ ] Cornichons, honey, whole-grain mustard
- [ ] Grapes or dried fruit

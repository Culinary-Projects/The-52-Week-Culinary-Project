# Session 10 — Roasting: Hot Oven, Dry Surface

[← Block 3–4: Heat & Cooking Methods Overview](index.md)

---

**Skill:** Roasting is dry oven heat that circulates around food, browning all exposed surfaces. The two secrets: (1) high heat (400°F+) and (2) a *dry* surface on the food. Pat everything dry. Never roast wet food — the moisture steams before it can brown.

**Full Meal:** [Oven-Roasted Thick-Cut Pork Chops](https://www.seriouseats.com/oven-roasted-pork-chops-recipe) + [Roasted Broccoli](https://www.seriouseats.com/the-best-roasted-broccoli-recipe) + Arugula Salad with Shaved Parmesan and Lemon (toss arugula with olive oil, lemon juice, shaved parm — done)

Use bone-in, thick-cut pork chops (1.5" minimum). Pat them completely dry, season generously. This is also the first time you'll use the pork internal temperature target: **145°F** — significantly lower than poultry's 165°F because the risk profile is different. Use your thermometer.

| Component | Recipe |
|-----------|--------|
| Protein | [Bone-in pork chops](https://www.seriouseats.com/oven-roasted-pork-chops-recipe), roasted until golden with a juicy interior — your first time hitting the 145°F pork target |
| Veg | [Roasted broccoli](https://www.seriouseats.com/the-best-roasted-broccoli-recipe) — high heat, single layer, tossed in olive oil; the florets should char at the tips |
| Salad | Arugula with shaved parmesan and lemon — peppery, sharp, simple |

> 🎥 **Compare Notes:** [How to Make Pan-Roasted Pork Chops](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HNBdnMKRjEU) — Notice how he starts on the stovetop for the sear, then finishes in the oven. A combination technique you'll use constantly.

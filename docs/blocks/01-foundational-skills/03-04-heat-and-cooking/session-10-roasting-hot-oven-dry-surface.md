# Session 10 — Roasting: Hot Oven, Dry Surface

[← Block 3–4: Heat & Cooking Methods Overview](index.md)

---

**Skill:** Roasting is dry oven heat that circulates around food, browning all exposed surfaces. The two secrets: (1) high heat (400°F+) and (2) a *dry* surface on the food. Pat everything dry. Never roast wet food — the moisture steams before it can brown.

**Full Meal:** [Perfect Pan-Seared Pork Chops](https://www.seriouseats.com/perfect-pan-seared-pork-chop-recipe) with Apple-Shallot Pan Sauce + [Easy Roasted Broccoli](https://www.seriouseats.com/easy-roasted-broccoli-recipe)

Use bone-in, thick-cut pork chops (1.5" minimum). Pat them completely dry, season generously. This is also the first time you'll use the pork internal temperature target: **145°F** — significantly lower than poultry's 165°F because the risk profile is different. Use your thermometer.

| Component | Recipe |
|-----------|--------|
| Protein | [Bone-in pork chops](https://www.seriouseats.com/perfect-pan-seared-pork-chop-recipe) — Kenji's method starts low in the oven, then sears in a hot pan. Your first time hitting the 145°F pork target |
| Veg | [Roasted broccoli](https://www.seriouseats.com/easy-roasted-broccoli-recipe) — 500°F oven, single layer, tossed in olive oil; the florets should char at the tips |
| Sauce | **Apple-shallot pan sauce** — after the pork rests, keep the fond in the pan. Sauté 1 sliced shallot and 1 sliced apple in the pork fat until softened. Deglaze with a splash of cider vinegar, add a knob of butter, swirl until glossy. Spoon over the chops. |

> 🎥 **Compare Notes:** [Pork Chops with Madeira Mustard Pan Sauce](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uLaaNEr1Lq4) *(Helen Rennie)* — Different approach to the same cut. Watch how she builds a pan sauce after the sear — you're doing the same thing here with apples and cider vinegar instead of Madeira and mustard. You'll go deeper on pan sauces in Block 5–6.

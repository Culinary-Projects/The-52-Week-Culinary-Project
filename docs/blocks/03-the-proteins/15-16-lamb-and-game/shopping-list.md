# 🛒 Shopping List — Block 15–16: Lamb & Game

← [Back to Full Guide](index.md) | [Back to Quick Ref](quick-ref.md)

---

> **How to use this list:** Block 15 is lamb from quick-cook (chops, rack) to slow (shoulder). Block 16 is braises and duck. Buy fresh lamb chops and duck breasts the day before or day-of. Lamb shoulder and shanks may need to be ordered from a butcher — call ahead. Duck legs for confit should be bought Thursday if you plan a weekend cook.

---

## ⚠️ Equipment Check

- [ ] **Instant-read thermometer** — lamb is served pink; you need a thermometer
- [ ] **Cast iron skillet or grill pan** — chops, rack sear, duck breast
- [ ] **Large Dutch oven (5–7 qt)** — both braises (shanks, navarin)
- [ ] **Wire rack + half-sheet pan** — elevated roasting for the rack of lamb

---

## 🥩 Proteins — Block 15

- [ ] **Lamb rib chops** — 4–6 chops, 1 inch thick (Session 66). Dry brine overnight if time allows.
- [ ] **Rack of lamb** — 1 full rack (8 ribs), Frenched or French-it-yourself (Session 67). May need to order from a butcher.
- [ ] **Ground lamb** — 1 lb, 80–85% lean (Session 68 kofta). Avoid extra-lean — fat = flavour.
- [ ] **Bone-in lamb shoulder** — 3–4 lbs (Session 69 slow roast). Call your butcher ahead; shoulder is rarely on display.

---

## 🥩 Proteins — Block 16

- [ ] **Lamb shanks** — 2 large or 4 small; hind shanks preferred (Session 70). 2.5–3 hours to braise.
- [ ] **Bone-in lamb neck or shoulder cut into stew pieces** — 1.5 lbs (Session 71 navarin)
- [ ] **Duck legs** — 4 legs with thighs attached, skin on (Session 72 confit). Asian markets and specialty butchers reliable. Buy Thursday for a weekend cook.
- [ ] **Duck breasts** — 2 magret-style breasts, skin on (Session 73). Larger breasts are easier — smaller ones overcook quickly.

---

## 🧅 Aromatics & Produce

- [ ] Garlic — 2 heads
- [ ] Fresh rosemary — large bunch (chops, rack crust, navarin)
- [ ] Fresh thyme — large bunch (braises, confit)
- [ ] Fresh flat-leaf parsley — navarin, gremolata
- [ ] Lemons — 4 (gremolata, tzatziki)
- [ ] White onions — 4 (navarin and braise base)
- [ ] Carrots — 6 (navarin and braise base)
- [ ] Celery — 1 bunch (braise base)
- [ ] Turnips or parsnips (optional, navarin)
- [ ] Fresh peas or haricots verts — navarin finishing, salad sides
- [ ] Cherry tomatoes — 1 pint

## 🥛 Dairy

- [ ] Duck fat — 1 jar (~14 oz); or render from extra duck legs bought ahead
- [ ] Greek yogurt — 16 oz (tzatziki for Session 68, sauce for shoulder)
- [ ] Unsalted butter — 2 sticks (pan sauces, duck breast finish)

## 🍷 Wine & Cooking Liquids

- [ ] Dry red wine — 1 bottle (braised shanks; any Côtes du Rhône or similar)
- [ ] Dry white wine — 1 bottle (navarin and vegetables)
- [ ] Chicken or lamb stock — 32 oz (braise base for Sessions 6 and 7)

## 🧂 Pantry

- [ ] Dijon mustard — rack crust and vinaigrette
- [ ] Panko breadcrumbs — rack herb crust
- [ ] Dried cumin, coriander, cinnamon, allspice — kofta spice blend
- [ ] Flat pita or lavash — kofta wraps (Session 68)
- [ ] Polenta (coarse-ground) — Session 70 side

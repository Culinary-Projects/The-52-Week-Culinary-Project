# 🛒 Shopping List — Block 17–18: Beef Fundamentals

← [Back to Full Guide](index.md) | [Back to Quick Ref](quick-ref.md)

---

> **How to use this list:** Block 15 is all about precision and high heat. Block 16 is braises — patient and hands-off. Buy your Block 15 ribeye on so you can dry brine it night for . The standing rib roast (Block 15) needs to be ordered from a butcher if you're not in a good-stocked area. The brisket (Block 16) needs to start by 11am if you're eating dinner.

---

## ⚠️ Equipment Check

- [ ] **Instant-read thermometer** — non-negotiable this block. You are cooking to temperature, not to time. Under $30 for a Thermapen-style; Lavatools or ThermoPop work great.
- [ ] **Cast iron skillet** — essential for // steaks. The heavier the better.
- [ ] **Large Dutch oven** — needed for all three braises (//, Block 16). 5–7 quart.
- [ ] **Wire rack** — for the reverse sear setup (steak on rack in a sheet pan in the oven)

---

## 🥩 Proteins — Block 15

- [ ] **Ribeye steak** — 1.5–2 inch thick; bone-in or boneless (dry brine/reverse sear). Buy, dry brine immediately.
- [ ] **Strip steaks** — 2 pieces, 1 inch thick (pan sear)
- [ ] **Ground beef** — 80/20, NOT lean; 1.5 lbs (smash burgers)
- [ ] **Skirt steak** — 1–1.5 lbs; inside skirt preferred (marinate overnight)
- [ ] **Standing rib roast (prime rib)** — 2-bone minimum; USDA Choice or Prime. Order ahead from a butcher.

---

## 🥩 Proteins — Block 16

- [ ] **Bone-in beef short ribs** — English cut; 3 lbs (braise)
- [ ] **Chuck roast** — 3 lbs, bone-in preferred (pot roast)
- [ ] **Brisket** — flat or point; 3–4 lbs, USDA Choice minimum (; start early)

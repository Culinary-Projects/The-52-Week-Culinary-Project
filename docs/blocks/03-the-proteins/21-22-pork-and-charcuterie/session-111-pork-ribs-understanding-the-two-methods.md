**Skill:** Spare ribs (more fat, more flavor) vs. baby back ribs (more tender, faster). Tonight: a hybrid method — 2 hours low in the oven, then a finish on the grill or under the broiler. This solves the classic rib problem: you need collagen conversion (time + moderate heat) AND caramelized exterior (high heat).

- **Read:** [Serious Eats — Sous Vide Pork Ribs](https://www.seriouseats.com/sous-vide-pork-ribs-recipe-food-lab) — even if you're not sous viding, the texture targets and timing are instructive

**Full Meal:** Ribs (your choice of spare or baby back) + [coleslaw](https://www.seriouseats.com/creamy-cole-slaw) + [cornbread](https://www.seriouseats.com/moist-and-tender-brown-butter-cornbread-recipe) or [baked beans](https://www.seriouseats.com/boston-baked-beans-recipe)

---

> 🎥 **Compare Notes:** [How to Cook Ribs in the Oven — Brian Lagerstrom](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=skXvtGgRQKw) — Brian's oven-to-finish rib method with vinegar slaw. Compare your bark, tenderness, and sauce glaze to his.

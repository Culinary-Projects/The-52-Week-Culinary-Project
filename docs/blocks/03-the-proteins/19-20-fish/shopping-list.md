# 🛒 Shopping List — Block 19–20: Seafood Fish

← [Back to Full Guide](index.md) | [Back to Quick Ref](quick-ref.md)

---

> **The golden rule of fish shopping:** Buy it day-of or the day before. Not earlier. Fish quality degrades faster than any other protein. If your usual grocery store doesn't have great seafood, find a fishmonger. Tell them you're cooking fish five nights a week for two weeks — they will help you figure out timing.

---

## ⚠️ Equipment Check

- [ ] **Dutch oven** — for 's fish tacos (frying)
- [ ] **Thermometer** — essential; also useful for checking oil temp for frying (350°F target)
- [ ] **Fish spatula** — wide, thin, flexible; the tool that makes flipping fish easy
- [ ] **Wire rack + sheet pan** — for draining fried fish tacos (Block 22)
- [ ] **Coarse sea salt** — 3–4 lbs if doing the salt-bake version of 's whole fish

---

## 🐟 Proteins — Block 21 (buy as close to cook day as possible)

- [ ] **Salmon fillets, skin-on** — 2 thick pieces ; 2 more pieces (miso glaze)
- [ ] **Dover sole or flounder fillets** — 2–4 (meunière)
- [ ] **Fresh white fish fillet** — cod, halibut, or sea bass; 2 pieces (technique practice)
- [ ] **Whole fish** — 1–2 branzino, snapper, or sea bass, 500g–1kg each (; buy an extra for Session 0 filleting practice if doing it)

---

## 🐟 Proteins — Block 22 (buy as close to cook day as possible)

- [ ] **Firm white fish** — cod or halibut, 2 pieces (mushroom cook)
- [ ] **White fish for frying** — cod, mahi-mahi, or tilapia; 1 lb (tacos)
- [ ] **Salmon fillets** — 2 pieces (teriyaki)
- [ ] **Branzino or snapper, whole** — 2 small (grill)
- [ ] **Bouillabaisse assortment** — 2–3 types: e.g., cod (1 lb), monkfish (1 lb) + 1 lb clams or mussels ; ask fishmonger to save bones/heads for the broth

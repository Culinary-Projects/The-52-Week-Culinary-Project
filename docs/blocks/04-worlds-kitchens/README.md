# Module 4: The World's Kitchens (Blocks 31–48)


---

> **The goal of this module:** Take every technique you've built and apply it through seven distinct culinary traditions. Each two-week immersion goes beyond recipes — you'll study the flavor logic, pantry structure, and cultural context that makes each cuisine a coherent system.

By the end of Week 48, you should be able to cook with the grammar of eight world cuisines, not just their recipes.

## Core Skills Covered

- **French** — classical brigade technique; beurre blanc, roux-based soups, coq au vin, tarte Tatin, proper knife work in a French context
- **Italian** — handmade pasta shapes beyond tagliatelle; risotto and polenta deep-dives; soffritto; regional sauce logic
- **Japanese** — dashi and umami foundation; teriyaki; ramen tare system; tempura batter technique; sushi rice and maki rolling; gyoza steam-fry
- **Korean** — kimchi and fermentation logic; banchan construction; gochujang and doenjang pantry; Korean fried chicken; galbi-jjim braising; pajeon technique
- **Chinese** — wok hei and high-heat stir-fry; velveting proteins; red-braising (hong shao); Cantonese steaming; Sichuan flavor logic; congee
- **Mexican** — dried chili taxonomy and toasting; masa and tortilla press; mole foundations; tamales; acid balance with lime
- **Indian** — whole spice blooming; wet and dry masalas; dal technique; tandoor-style high-heat cooking; yogurt-based marinades
- **Middle Eastern** — mezze construction; labneh and dairy ferments; sumac and pomegranate molasses as finishing acids; slow-roasted lamb
- **Southeast Asian** — wok technique (high heat, short time); curry paste from scratch; fish sauce as seasoning backbone; larb and acid-balance

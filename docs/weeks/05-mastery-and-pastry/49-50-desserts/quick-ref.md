## Custard & Set Dessert Reference

| Dessert | Set By | Eggs | Liquid | Temp | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Crème Brûlée | Heat (egg yolk) | 5 yolks | 500ml cream | 325°F, bake to 160°F | Bain-marie; wobble test |
| Panna Cotta | Gelatin | none | 500ml cream + milk | No bake | 1 tsp gelatin per 2 cups; bloom first |
| Pastry Cream | Heat + starch (yolks) | 4 yolks | 500ml milk | Boil then pull | Plastic wrap on surface; refrigerate |
| Crème Anglaise | Heat (yolk) | 4 yolks | 500ml milk/cream | 180°F, coat spoon | Strain; do NOT boil |
| Chocolate Mousse | Air (whites) | 3 whites | — | Cool chocolate first | Fold in large strokes; chill 2h |
| Tiramisu | Cold (mascarpone) | 4 yolks | — | No bake | 4h minimum; overnight best |

## Gelatin Reference

| Form | Conversion | Bloom Process | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Powdered | 1 packet = 2¼ tsp = 7g | Sprinkle over cold water; wait 5 min | Use 1 tsp per 2 cups for soft set |
| Sheet (silver) | 1 sheet ≈ 1 tsp powdered | Submerge in cold water 5 min; squeeze dry | More consistent; professional standard |

**Panna cotta gel strength:** 1 tsp powdered = just-holds shape; 1½ tsp = firmer; 2 tsp = bouncy (too much)

## Sugar Stages Reference

| Stage | Temperature | Appearance | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Thread | 230–235°F | Thin threads | Simple syrups |
| Soft ball | 235–240°F | Soft pliable ball | Fondant, fudge |
| Firm ball | 245–250°F | Holds shape | Caramel candies |
| Hard ball | 250–265°F | Rigid ball | Nougat |
| Soft crack | 270–290°F | Threads, brittle | Butterscotch |
| Hard crack | 300–310°F | Glassy, shatters | Toffee, brittles |
| Caramel | 320–360°F | Golden to dark amber | Caramel sauce |

## Pavlova / Meringue Reference

| Type | Sugar Ratio | Technique | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| French Meringue | 2:1 sugar:white | Whip to stiff, fold granulated sugar | Pavlova, macarons |
| Swiss Meringue | 2:1 over warm water | Warm + whip; silky stable | Buttercream, toasts |
| Italian Meringue | Hot syrup 240°F into whites | Most stable | Baked Alaska, pastries |

**Pavlova specifics:**
- Vinegar + cornstarch added → marshmallow interior guaranteed
- Bake at 250°F for 90–120 minutes; turn off oven and leave inside until cool
- Top immediately before serving (whipped cream softens the crust after 30 min)
- Humidity is the enemy of meringue; don't bake on rainy days

## Caramel vs. Butterscotch vs. Toffee

| Name | Fat | Stage | Texture |
|---|---|---|---|
| Caramel sauce | Butter + cream added after amber | 340–360°F | Pourable sauce |
| Butterscotch sauce | Brown sugar + butter cooked together | Lower (soft ball) | Butterier, richer |
| Toffee | Higher butter ratio, cooked to hard crack | 300–310°F | Solid, brittle |
| Sticky toffee sauce | Butter + cream + brown sugar | Boiled together | Pourable, used warm |

## Chocolate Chip Cookie Variables

| Variable | Effect on Result |
|---|---|
| Bread flour vs. AP | More chew, denser structure |
| Brown butter | Nuttier, more complex flavor |
| More brown sugar | Chewier, more moist |
| More white sugar | Crispier, spreads more |
| Overnight chill | Drier dough, concentrated flavor, better browning |
| Sea salt on top | Flavor contrast; brightens sweetness |
| Higher bake temp | Crispier exterior; less set interior |

## Layer Cake Technique

1. **Weigh batter** into even layers (don't eyeball)
2. **Bake with cake strips** (wet strips around pan = even rise, no dome)
3. **Cool completely** before any frosting contact
4. **Level tops** with serrated knife
5. **Moistening syrup** (simple syrup + flavoring) brushed on each layer
6. **Crumb coat** — thin layer, refrigerate 15–20 minutes
7. **Final coat** — smooth with bench scraper
8. **Refrigerate** assembled cake; bring to temp 30 minutes before serving

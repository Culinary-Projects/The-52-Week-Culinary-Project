## Japanese Pantry Reference

| Ingredient | Role | Substitute |
|------------|------|------------|
| [Dashi](https://www.seriouseats.com/basic-japanese-dashi-recipe) | Umami-rich stock base | Kombu dashi (vegan) or dashi powder |
| Mirin | Sweet rice wine (cooking) | Sake + pinch of sugar |
| Sake | Dry rice wine (cooking) | Dry sherry or white wine |
| Soy sauce | Salinity + umami | Tamari (gluten-free) |
| [Miso (white)](https://www.seriouseats.com/15-minute-miso-butter-ramen-recipe-11907783) | Mild, sweet fermented paste | Mixed or red miso (stronger flavor) |
| Panko | [Light, airy Japanese breadcrumb](https://www.seriouseats.com/basic-japanese-dashi-recipe) | Homemade coarse crumbs (not the same) |
| Rice vinegar | Mild acid for seasoning | White wine vinegar (use less) |

## Key Temperature Reference

| Technique | Temp | Notes |
|-----------|------|-------|
| Kombu steep | 140°F / 60°C | Never boil — activates bitter compounds |
| [Tonkatsu fry](https://www.seriouseats.com/tonkatsu-chicken-pork-katsu-japanese-breaded-cutlets-recipe) | 350°F / 176°C | Test with a crumb; should sizzle |
| [Tempura fry](https://www.seriouseats.com/deep-fried-green-bean-and-shiitake-tempura-with-ponzu-mayonnaise-recipe) | 360–375°F | Higher than tonkatsu; batter is thinner |
| Soft-boiled ramen egg | 6.5 min boil, ice bath | Jammy yolk; marinate overnight |

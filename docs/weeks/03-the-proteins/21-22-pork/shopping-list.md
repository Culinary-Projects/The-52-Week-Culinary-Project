# 🛒 Shopping List — Weeks 19–20: Pork All Cuts

← [Back to Full Guide](index.md) | [Back to Quick Ref](quick-ref.md)

---

> **How to use this list:** Two major project cooks this block — the slow-roasted pork shoulder (Week 19 Sat, start by 11 AM) and carnitas (Week 20 Sat, start by noon). Carnitas ingredients are simple; don't overthink the shopping. The porchetta (Week 20 Wed) should be assembled and dried uncovered in the fridge at least overnight — prep it Monday or Tuesday.

---

## ⚠️ Equipment Check

- [ ] **Dutch oven** (5–7 quart) — essential for chashu, carnitas, meatballs
- [ ] **Kitchen twine** — for rolling and tying porchetta
- [ ] **Wire rack + sheet pan setup** — pork shoulder needs to sit on a rack in a shallow pan
- [ ] **Instant-read thermometer** — non-negotiable for pork (145°F target)

---

## 🥩 Proteins — Week 19

- [ ] **Bone-in rib chops** — 1 inch thick; 1 per person (Mon; dry brine Sun night)
- [ ] **Pork belly (skin-on, slab)** — 2 lbs (Tue chashu; buy early for prep)
- [ ] **Pork tenderloin** — 1–1.5 lbs (Wed; often sold in 2-pack)
- [ ] **Bone-in pork chops, thin** — ½ inch; 2–3 pieces (Fri Vietnamese grill; marinate Thu night)
- [ ] **Bone-in pork shoulder** — 4–5 lbs with fat cap (Sat; buy Fri to dry brine overnight)

---

## 🥩 Proteins — Week 20

- [ ] **Pork spare ribs or baby back** — 1 full rack (Tue)
- [ ] **Pork belly (skin-on)** — 2 lbs (Wed porchetta; buy Mon and prep immediately)
- [ ] **Ground pork** — 1 lb (Fri meatballs)
- [ ] **Pork shoulder** — 4 lbs boneless (Sat carnitas)

## Module 1: Foundational Skills

### Weeks 1–2: Knife Skills

**Session 1 — The Pinch Grip**  
*Pasta with Butter and Parmesan*  
- **Main:** Parmesan-crusted pasta
- **Veg:** Wilted baby spinach, sautéed in the pasta pan with olive oil, garlic, and lemon

**Session 2 — The Claw**  
*The Best Chicken Noodle Soup*  
- **Protein:** Chicken breast — poach in the broth (or sous vide if you have one), then dice
- **Veg:** Onion (charred, per recipe) into the stock; your diced carrot and celery into the soup for last 10 minutes
- **Starch:** Egg noodles, cooked seperately

**Session 3 — Rock Chop vs. Push Cut**  
*Shrimp Scampi*  
- **Protein:** Shrimp, sautéed in garlic butter and white wine
- **Veg/Salad:** Arugula, Fennel & Orange Salad
- **Starch:** Crusty bread or linguine

**Session 4 — Mise en Place**  
*Stir-Fried Beef and Broccoli*  
- **Protein:** Thinly sliced flank steak, seared in very small batches
- **Veg:** Broccoli florets, seared in the same pan
- **Starch:** Steamed jasmine rice, started before you begin the stir-fry

**Session 5 — PROJECT MEAL: French Onion Soup**  
*French Onion Soup (Soupe à l'Oignon)*  

**Session 6 — The Julienne**  
*The Best Pad Thai*  
- **Protein:** Shrimp, chicken, or tofu
- **Veg:** Your julienned carrots and scallions, plus bean sprouts; finish with fresh basil chiffonade
- **Starch:** Rice noodles, soaked per the recipe

**Session 7 — The Dice (Small, Medium, Large)**  
*Home Fries + Pan-Seared Crispy Chicken Thighs*  
- **Protein:** Crispy pan-seared bone-in chicken thighs
- **Veg/Salad:** Classic Green Salad with Vinaigrette
- **Starch:** Home fries (your dice drill is the recipe)

**Session 8 — Breaking Down a Whole Chicken**  
*Chicken Piccata (use the breasts) + Burst Cherry Tomatoes*  
- **Protein:** Chicken piccata using the breasts you broke down
- **Veg:** Burst Cherry Tomatoes with Garlic and Olive Oil
- **Starch:** Linguine — toss with the burst tomatoes and a splash of pasta water

**Session 9 — Speed Benchmark**  
*Shakshuka + leftover chicken from earlier in the block thighs (warmed in a skillet)*  
- **Protein:** Eggs poached in the shakshuka sauce + leftover chicken from earlier in the block thighs
- **Veg:** Onion, bell pepper, tomatoes (the shakshuka base)
- **Starch:** Crusty bread for dipping

**Session 10 — PROJECT MEAL: Provençal Vegetable Tian**  
*Provençal Summer Vegetable Tian + crusty bread*  
- **Protein:** Optional — serve with good burrata or a soft-boiled egg on top
- **Veg:** The tian (eggplant, zucchini, squash, tomato)
- **Starch:** Crusty baguette

### Weeks 3–4: Heat And Cooking

**Session 1 — Sauté: Hot Pan, Don't Crowd**  
*Sautéed Chicken Breasts with Quick Pan Sauce + Sautéed Zucchini with Garlic and Herbs + Steamed Rice*  
- **Protein:** Sautéed boneless chicken breast, finished with a quick pan sauce of shallot, white wine, and butter
- **Veg:** Sautéed zucchini — notice how a hot pan gives you color on the outside while keeping the inside tender
- **Starch:** Steamed jasmine rice

**Session 2 — Roasting: Hot Oven, Dry Surface**  
*Sheet Pan Chicken Thighs with Roasted Vegetables + Simple Green Salad*  
- **Protein:** Bone-in skin-on chicken thighs, roasted until crackling
- **Veg:** Whatever is on the sheet pan — broccoli, potatoes, onions
- **Salad:** Simple green salad alongside

**Session 3 — Braising: Low, Slow, Wet**  
*Braised Chicken Thighs with Tomatoes and Capers + Crusty bread or polenta*  
- **Protein:** Braised chicken thighs in a rich tomato-caper sauce
- **Starch:** Crusty bread to mop the sauce, or polenta

**Session 4 — Steaming and Poaching: Gentle, Wet Heat**  
*Perfect Poached Salmon + Steamed Bok Choy with Garlic Sauce + Steamed rice*  
- **Protein:** Gently poached salmon — silky, delicate, nothing like a seared fillet
- **Veg:** Steamed bok choy, dressed simply with garlic and sesame
- **Starch:** Steamed rice

**Session 5 — PROJECT MEAL: Spatchcocked Roast Chicken**  
*Spatchcocked Roast Chicken + Roasted Potatoes + Simple Green Salad with Vinaigrette*  

**Session 6 — Searing Steak: The Art of Doing Nothing**  
*Perfect Pan-Seared Steak with Butter Basting + Creamy Mashed Potatoes + Simple Sautéed Green Beans*  
- **Protein:** Ribeye or NY strip, pan-seared in cast iron, basted in butter, thyme, and garlic
- **Starch:** Ultra-creamy mashed potatoes (use more butter than you're comfortable with)
- **Veg:** Green beans sautéed in the steak pan drippings

**Session 7 — High-Heat Fish: Crispy Skin Without Fear**  
*Crispy Pan-Seared Salmon + Roasted Asparagus + Simple Arugula and Fennel Salad*  
- **Protein:** Crispy-skinned salmon fillet, finished with lemon and capers
- **Veg:** Roasted asparagus (high-heat oven, single layer, very dry)
- **Salad:** Arugula salad alongside

**Session 8 — Broiling: Overhead Heat**  
*Broiled Salmon with Miso Glaze + Sautéed Bok Choy + Steamed rice*  
- **Protein:** Miso-glazed salmon under the broiler — 8 minutes, no flipping needed
- **Veg:** Sautéed or steamed bok choy
- **Starch:** Steamed rice

**Session 9 — Recap Cook: No Recipes**  
*Braised Short Ribs + Creamy Polenta + Roasted Root Vegetables*  
- **Protein:** Bone-in beef short ribs braised in red wine and beef stock until they collapse

### Weeks 7–8: Stocks And Eggs

**Session 0 — Soup Architecture *(optional)***  
*Roasted Butternut Squash Soup + crusty bread + simple salad*  
- **Family:** Technique
- ****Broth/consommé**:** Clear liquid, flavored by solids
- ****Purée**:** Cooked vegetables blended smooth; no cream needed
- ****Cream soup**:** Purée + cream or béchamel for richness and texture
- ****Bisque**:** Shellfish shells simmered into the base; always cream-finished
- ****Chowder**:** Chunky; potato base; salt pork/bacon; cream-finished

### Weeks 11–12: Vegetables Plant Forward

**Session 0 — Two Purées, One Method *(optional)***  
*Celery root and parsnip purées as the base + seared duck breast or seared scallops on top + a simple green salad*  
- **Purée:** Method notes
- ****Celery root (celeriac)**:** Sweat in butter, add stock, simmer until completely soft, blend very fine, strain
- ****Parsnip with roasted garlic**:** Roast garlic until golden; simmer parsnips in stock; blend with roasted garlic and butter

**Session 1 — Blanching, Shocking, and Color Science**  
*Blanched and shocked asparagus + haricots verts + broccolini — dressed with brown butter and toasted almonds, served at room temperature*  
- **Pigment:** Vegetable examples
- ****Chlorophyll** (green):** Broccoli, asparagus, spinach, peas
- ****Anthocyanin** (red/purple):** Red cabbage, purple carrots, beets
- ****Carotenoid** (orange/yellow):** Carrots, squash, sweet potato
- ****Betanin** (deep red):** Beets
- **Vegetables:** Asparagus, haricots verts, broccolini — all blanched and shocked
- **Dressing:** Brown butter + toasted almonds + lemon zest + fleur de sel
- **Protein (optional):** Soft-boiled eggs on top to make it a dinner

**Session 2 — Roasting: Caramelization and Concentration**  
*Sheet Pan Dinner — roasted carrots, cauliflower, and beets with whatever protein you prefer + a tahini dressing or romesco*  
- **Vegetables:** Carrots (halved), cauliflower (florets), golden beets (quartered)
- **Dressing:** Tahini sauce or romesco
- **Protein:** Chicken thighs on the same pan, or eat vegetarian

**Session 3 — Sauté, Braise, and the Crucifer Redemption**  
*- Crispy Brussels Sprouts with Fish Sauce and Crispy Shallots — this dish converts Brussels sprout haters*  
- **Brussels sprouts:** Halved, pressed cut-side into very hot oil; don't move; then toss
- **Cabbage:** Wedges, cast iron, very high heat; miso glaze brushed at the end

**Session 4 — Grilling and Charring: Fire as Flavor**  
*A grilled and charred vegetable spread:*

- **Eggplant:** Full char over gas flame or under broiler; peel and season
- **Zucchini:** Direct grill, slight char marks, finish with lemon + mint
- **Scallions:** Whole on the grill until charred and collapsed
- **Peppers:** Full char, steam in bag, peel and seed

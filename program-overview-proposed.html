    The 52-Week Culinary Project — Proposed Program Overview

    This document reflects the curriculum as it would look after all proposals in curriculum-proposals.md are implemented. Changes are noted with [CHANGED], [NEW], or [REMOVED] tags. No curriculum files have been modified.

    Module 1: Foundational Skills

    Weeks 1–2: Knife Skills (unchanged)

    Session 1 — The Pinch Grip Pasta with Butter and Parmesan - Main: Parmesan-crusted pasta - Veg: Wilted baby spinach, sautéed in the pasta pan with olive oil, garlic, and lemon

    Session 2 — The Claw The Best Chicken Noodle Soup - Protein: Chicken breast, poached in the broth, then diced - Veg: Charred onion into the stock; diced carrot and celery added for last 10 minutes - Starch: Egg noodles, cooked separately

    Session 3 — Rock Chop vs. Push Cut Shrimp Scampi - Protein: Shrimp, sautéed in garlic butter and white wine - Veg/Salad: Arugula, fennel, and orange salad - Starch: Crusty bread or linguine

    Session 4 — Mise en Place Stir-Fried Beef and Broccoli - Protein: Thinly sliced flank steak, seared in small batches - Veg: Broccoli florets, seared in the same pan - Starch: Steamed jasmine rice

    Session 5 — PROJECT MEAL: French Onion Soup French Onion Soup (Soupe à l'Oignon)

    Session 6 — The Julienne The Best Pad Thai - Protein: Shrimp, chicken, or tofu - Veg: Julienned carrots and scallions, bean sprouts, fresh basil chiffonade - Starch: Rice noodles

    Session 7 — The Dice (Small, Medium, Large) Home Fries + Pan-Seared Crispy Chicken Thighs - Protein: Crispy pan-seared bone-in chicken thighs - Veg/Salad: Warm green bean and walnut salad [CHANGED from simple green salad] - Starch: Home fries (the dice drill is the recipe)

    Session 8 — Breaking Down a Whole Chicken Chicken Piccata + Burst Cherry Tomatoes - Protein: Chicken piccata using the breasts you broke down - Veg: Burst cherry tomatoes with garlic and olive oil - Starch: Linguine tossed with the burst tomatoes

    Session 9 — Speed Benchmark Shakshuka - Protein: Eggs poached in the shakshuka sauce - Veg: Onion, bell pepper, tomatoes (the shakshuka base) - Starch: Crusty bread for dipping

    Session 10 — PROJECT MEAL: Provençal Vegetable Tian Provençal Summer Vegetable Tian - Protein: Optional — burrata or soft-boiled egg on top - Veg: The tian (eggplant, zucchini, squash, tomato) - Starch: Fougasse or crusty baguette [CHANGED from plain crusty bread]

    Weeks 3–4: Heat & Cooking (Proposal K: en papillote added)

    Session 1 — Sauté: Hot Pan, Don't Crowd Sautéed Chicken Breasts with Quick Pan Sauce + Sautéed Zucchini - Protein: Sautéed boneless chicken breast, finished with a quick pan sauce of shallot, white wine, and butter - Veg: Sautéed zucchini with garlic - Starch: Rice pilaf with toasted vermicelli [CHANGED from plain steamed rice]

    Session 2 — Roasting: Hot Oven, Dry Surface Sheet Pan Chicken Thighs with Roasted Vegetables - Protein: Bone-in skin-on chicken thighs, roasted until crackling - Veg: Sheet pan vegetables (broccoli, onions) - Salad: Panzanella with torn croutons [CHANGED from simple green salad]

    Session 3 — Braising: Low, Slow, Wet Braised Chicken Thighs with Tomatoes and Capers - Protein: Braised chicken thighs in a rich tomato-caper sauce - Starch: Creamy polenta [CHANGED from crusty bread]

    Session 4 — Steaming, Poaching & En Papillote [CHANGED — expanded from "Gentle, Wet Heat"] Sea Bass en Papillote + Poached Chicken Breast in Court Bouillon - Protein: Sea bass packet (herbs, lemon, capers) — en papillote; poached chicken breast in court bouillon - Veg: Steam-wilted spinach with lemon - Starch: Plain white rice to contrast

    Session 5 — PROJECT MEAL: Spatchcocked Roast Chicken Spatchcocked Roast Chicken - Protein: Spatchcocked whole chicken - Veg: Roasted carrots with honey and thyme [CHANGED from roasted potatoes] - Salad: Simple green salad with vinaigrette

    Session 6 — Searing Steak: The Art of Doing Nothing Pan-Seared Ribeye + Pan Sauce - Protein: Ribeye, seared in cast iron - Starch: Pommes Anna [CHANGED — introduces potato technique early] - Veg: Roasted asparagus

    Session 7 — High-Heat Fish: Crispy Skin [CHANGED — no longer the salmon-with-nothing recipe] Pan-Seared Branzino with Brown Butter and Capers - Protein: Whole branzino fillet, skin-on, seared to glass - Veg: French green beans (haricots verts) with toasted almonds - Starch: Crusty bread

    Session 8 — Broiling: Overhead Heat Broiled Salmon with Herb Crust + Broiled Grapefruit Salad - Protein: Salmon fillet under the broiler - Veg/Salad: Warm grain salad with farro and roasted peppers [CHANGED]

    Session 9 — Recap Cook: No Recipes Multi-Method Dinner: learner's choice across all four methods

    Weeks 5–6: Mother Sauces (Proposals D, J: beurre blanc added; crispy salmon moved here as the one canonical occurrence)

    Session 1 — Béchamel: The White Sauce Croque Monsieur + Mornay-Dressed Broccoli - Protein: Ham and gruyère croque monsieur - Veg: Broccoli florets in Mornay sauce

    Session 2 — Velouté: Stock Instead of Milk Chicken Breast with Velouté + Steamed Vegetables - Protein: Sautéed chicken breast finished with chicken velouté - Veg: Steamed green beans and carrots - Starch: Rice pilaf [CHANGED from plain rice]

    Session 3 — Tomato Sauce: The Italian Mother [CHANGED — was missing in original; added to fill the gap] Pasta al Pomodoro + Burrata - Protein/Main: Hand-torn pasta in slow-cooked tomato sauce - Accompaniment: Fresh burrata, olive oil

    Session 4 — Espagnole and Demi-Glace (Simplified) Pan-Seared Duck Breast with Cherry Sauce [CHANGED — was vague; now a specific use-case] - Protein: Duck breast, rendered fat, demi-glace-based cherry reduction - Starch: Creamed lentils [CHANGED — introduces legumes early]

    Session 5 — Hollandaise: The Emulsion Eggs Benedict with Hollandaise - Protein: Poached eggs, Canadian bacon - Starch: English muffin - Salad: Arugula salad with lemon

    Session 6 — Beurre Blanc + Compound Butters [NEW — Proposal J] Pan-Seared Salmon with Beurre Blanc + Crispy Capers [THE ONE canonical crispy salmon — Proposal D] - Protein: Crispy skin-on salmon fillet — the definitive version - Sauce: Classic beurre blanc (white wine reduction + cold butter) - Veg: Steamed asparagus with maître d'hôtel butter - Starch: New potatoes with dill butter [CHANGED — uses compound butter just made]

    Session 7 — Mayonnaise: Emulsion Without Heat Niçoise Salad - Protein: Tuna (canned or seared), hard-boiled eggs - Veg: Green beans, tomatoes, olives, potatoes

    Session 8 — The Vinaigrette: A Temporary Emulsion Composed Salad with Warm Bacon Vinaigrette [CHANGED — illustrates warm vs. cold dressing] - Protein: Lardons, poached egg - Veg: Frisée and bitter greens - Starch: Torn croutons

    Session 9 — Pan Sauce: The Working Sauce Pork Chop with Sauce Robert [CHANGED — was generic; now teaches a specific CIA-classic derivative sauce] - Protein: Bone-in pork chop, fond-based sauce Robert (mustard, white wine) - Veg: Braised cabbage with caraway - Starch: Mashed potatoes

    Session 10 — PROJECT MEAL: Classic French Bistro Dinner (3 Courses) French onion soup → Chicken Chasseur → Tarte Tatin - First: French onion soup (contextually revisited from a sauce perspective) - Main: Chicken chasseur (hunter-style, demonstrates tomato-espagnole derivative) - Dessert: Tarte Tatin with crème fraîche

    Weeks 7–8: Stocks & Eggs (Proposals F, G: bouillabaisse stays; mushroom risotto replaced with Milanese; soup architecture promoted)

    Session 1 — Soup Architecture: The Five Families [PROMOTED from optional to Session 1 — Proposal H] Cream Soup (Broccoli) + Purée (Butternut Squash) + Cold Soup (Gazpacho) - Demonstrates: broth → cream → purée → bisque → chowder taxonomy in one session - Full meal: three mini soup courses, served in sequence

    Session 2 — Chicken Stock from Scratch Hearty Chicken Vegetable Soup (from the stock made today) - Main: Clear chicken broth with vegetables — tastes the difference between real stock and water

    Session 3 — Pan Sauces from Real Stock Roast Chicken Thighs + Three Pan Sauces from One Pan - Protein: Chicken thighs, three sauce variants from the same fond - Starch: Wheat berry pilaf [CHANGED — introduces whole grains]

    Session 4 — Fish Stock + Fumet Mussels Marinière (uses the fumet) + Grilled Bread - Protein: Mussels in white wine and fumet broth - Starch: Grilled baguette

    Session 5 — Vegetable Stock + Risotto Milanese [CHANGED — Proposal G: mushroom risotto replaced] Risotto Milanese (Saffron + Bone Marrow if available) - Main: Risotto alla Milanese — saffron-gold, enriched; this becomes the definitive risotto session

    Session 6 — PROJECT MEAL: A Whole Soup Kitchen Day + Bouillabaisse [THE ONE canonical bouillabaisse — Proposal F] Bouillabaisse with Rouille and Gruyère Toasts - Main: Full bouillabaisse — multiple fish, fennel, saffron broth - Accompaniment: Rouille on grilled bread

    Session 7 — The French Scramble Soft Scrambled Eggs on Toast + Simple Side Salad - Protein: Butter-finished scrambled eggs - Salad: Watercress and radish salad [CHANGED from green salad]

    Session 8 — The Perfect Poached Egg Eggs Florentine - Protein: Poached eggs on creamed spinach - Sauce: Hollandaise (review from 05-06)

    Session 9 — The Omelette Classic French Omelette + Côte de Porc - Protein: French omelette (technique focused — no color, soft curd) - Side: Small arugula salad with lemon

    Session 10 — Custard: Eggs as Thickener — Quiche Lorraine Quiche Lorraine (full savory custard) - Main: Quiche Lorraine with lardons and gruyère in pâte brisée - Salad: Frisée salad with mustard vinaigrette

    Session 11 — PROJECT MEAL: A Complete Egg Dinner Party (3 Courses) Leek and Gruyère Tart → Omelette Station → Crème Caramel

    Module 2: Building Flavor

    Weeks 9–10: Seasoning & Flavor (Proposals H, L: spice blends + soup architecture extended)

    Session 1 — Early Salt: Brining Dry-Brined Roast Chicken - Protein: 24-hour dry-brined whole chicken, roasted - Veg: Roasted root vegetables (carrots, parsnips) - Starch: Freekeh pilaf [CHANGED — introduces whole grain]

    Session 2 — During Salt: Layered Seasoning Pasta Carbonara — Season Every Step - Main: Carbonara — season pasta water, eggs, and finish - Salad: Shaved fennel and parmesan salad [CHANGED]

    Session 3 — Late Salt: Finishing Steak with Maldon + Vegetable Crudo - Protein: Seared steak finished with flaked salt - Veg: Raw vegetable crudo, finished salts demonstrated

    Session 4 — Salt as Texture and Contrast Dark Chocolate and Sea Salt Tasting + Salted Caramel Sauce - Focus: Salt amplifying sweetness — dessert context

    Session 5 — PROJECT MEAL: Salt-Crusted Whole Roasted Fish Salt-Crusted Sea Bass - Main: Whole fish in salt crust — aromatic herbs inside, cracked tableside - Veg: Roasted fennel and olives - Starch: Grilled ciabatta

    Session 6 — Acid: The Brightness Lever Fish Tacos with Three Acid Salsas - Protein: Fish tacos with lime crema - Condiments: Pico de gallo, pickled red onion, salsa verde — acid compared

    Session 7 — Different Acids, Different Effects Pan-Seared Chicken with Three Vinegar Pan Sauces - Protein: Chicken thigh, tested with sherry vinegar, rice vinegar, and balsamic

    Session 8 — Fat as Flavor Duck Fat Potatoes + Confit Garlic Butter Bread - Starch: Duck fat–roasted potatoes - Accompaniment: Bread with confit garlic butter

    Session 9 — Spice Blends, Dry Rubs & Marinades [NEW — Proposal L] Garam Masala–Rubbed Lamb Chops + Flatbread + Cucumber Raita - Focus: Build garam masala from scratch; construct a wet marinade (mojo citrus); construct a curry paste (red Thai base) - Protein: Lamb chops, dry-rubbed and grilled - Starch: Homemade flatbread - Sauce: Cucumber raita made during the session

    Session 10 — Umami: The Depth Dial Miso-Glazed Eggplant + Dashi Broth + Mushroom Toast - Focus: Fermented + glutamate umami sources: miso, Parmesan, anchovies, soy - Main: Miso-glazed eggplant over rice - Additional: Dashi broth tasting; mushroom toast with Parmesan gremolata

    Session 11 — PROJECT MEAL: A Composed Tasting Plate Five-bite tasting plate applying salt, acid, fat, umami, and spice systematically

    Weeks 11–12: Vegetables (Proposal I: potato technique promoted to full session)

    Session 1 — Potatoes: Five Techniques, One Vegetable [CHANGED — Proposal I; was optional or absent] Gratin Dauphinoise + Rösti + Whipped Potatoes — Three Versions, One Session - Family 1 (Mashed): Whipped potatoes with brown butter - Family 2 (Dry Heat): Rösti (pan-crispy, starch released) - Family 3 (Baked/Gratin): Gratin dauphinoise — cream, garlic, slow oven - Serve all three as a composed potato dinner

    Session 2 — Blanching, Shocking, and Color Science Blanched Vegetable Platter with Three Dipping Sauces - Veg: Asparagus, haricots verts, broccoli rabe — all blanched and shocked - Sauces: Warm anchovy butter, mustard vinaigrette, aioli

    Session 3 — Roasting: Caramelization and Concentration Honey-Roasted Carrots + Oven-Roasted Tomatoes + Curried Roasted Cauliflower - Focus: Three vegetables, three sugar/caramelization profiles

    Session 4 — Sauté, Braise, and the Crucifer Redemption Braised Red Cabbage + Pan-Sautéed Brussels Sprouts with Bacon - Veg: Two preparations, two textures — braised vs. sautéed crucifer

    Session 5 — Grilling and Charring: Fire as Flavor Grilled Vegetables Provençal-Style - Veg: Zucchini, peppers, eggplant, tomato — marinated and charred - Starch: Grilled flatbread

    Session 6 — PROJECT: Plant-Forward Dinner Party Three-course vegetable dinner: soup → main vegetable dish → dessert

    Session 7 — The Composed Salad: Dressing Is a Technique Warm Farro Salad + Three Vinaigrette Variants [CHANGED — was generic composed salad] - Focus: Emulsification ratio, warm vs. room temp dressings, grain-based salad - Main: Warm farro with roasted beets, goat cheese, walnuts — three different vinaigrettes tested

    Session 8 — Legumes and Beans: The Forgotten Protein [EXPANDED — Proposal: legumes as a technique] Falafel + White Bean Stew + Quick-Soaked vs. No-Soak Black Beans (tasting comparison) - Focus: Soaking vs. no-soak; canned vs. dried; mashed vs. whole; fried (falafel) - Main: Falafel plate with hummus, white bean stew on the side

    Session 9 — Mushrooms: Umami in Plant Form Pan-Roasted King Trumpet Mushrooms + Mushroom Duxelles on Toast - Main: Mixed mushroom dinner — sautéed, roasted, and duxelles variations - Starch: Grilled sourdough

    Session 10 — A Completely Vegetable-Driven Main Eggplant Parmesan [CHANGED — was vague; CIA-classic vegetable main] - Main: Eggplant parmesan — breaded, fried, baked in tomato sauce - Salad: Shaved fennel and citrus salad

    Session 11 — PROJECT: The Vegetable Tasting Menu Five-course vegetable dinner designed and executed by the learner

    Weeks 13–14: Pasta & Grains (Proposals E, G: osso buco replaces duplicate short ribs; duplicate mushroom risotto replaced)

    Session 2 — Ravioli: Pasta as a Vessel Ricotta Ravioli with Tomato-Butter Sauce [CHANGED sauce — removes duplicate brown butter ravioli] - Main: Ricotta and herb ravioli in a simple tomato-butter sauce

    Session 3 — Gnocchi: Potato and Restraint Gnocchi with Gorgonzola Cream Sauce [CHANGED — removes duplicate brown butter gnocchi] - Main: Light potato gnocchi, dolcelatte variation - Veg: Wilted spinach

    Session 4 — Pasta Water: The Secret Sauce Ingredient Cacio e Pepe (Master Level) [CHANGED — teaches pasta water emulsion in its purest form] - Main: Cacio e pepe — three ingredients, perfected through technique

    Session 5 — Osso Buco alla Milanese [NEW — Proposal E: replaces duplicate braised short ribs] Veal Cross-Cut Braised Over Risotto Milanese + Gremolata - Protein: Braised veal shank — teaches the same braising logic as short ribs but a new cut and preparation - Starch: Risotto Milanese (review from 07-08) - Finish: Gremolata (lemon zest, garlic, parsley) — classic finish

    Session 6 — Risotto: Patience as Technique [CHANGED — not mushroom risotto] Risotto alle Vongole (Clam Risotto) - Main: Risotto with clams and white wine — teaches risotto method applied to a seafood context - Veg: Grilled radicchio salad [CHANGED from plain salad]

    Session 7 — Flavored Pasta Doughs [NEW — fills CIA Ch25 gap; saffron, squid ink, beet] Saffron Pasta with Seafood + Squid Ink Pasta with Shrimp (tasting comparison) - Main: Two flavored pasta sheets rolled and cut — students taste and compare technique

    Session 8 — Spaetzle and Dumplings [NEW — fills CIA Ch25 gap] Spaetzle with Caramelized Onions and Gruyère - Main: Spaetzle — German egg dumpling, teaches batter-drop technique - Veg: Braised red cabbage

    Session 9 — Farro and Grains That Think for Themselves Farro Risotto with Mushrooms and Thyme - Main: Farrotto — whole grain cooked risotto-style - Salad: Arugula with lemon and parmesan

    Session 10 — PROJECT MEAL: A Grain-Forward Dinner Party Three-course grain dinner: grain soup → pasta/grain main → grain dessert (rice pudding)

    Session 10 — PROJECT: The Beef Dinner for Four Three-course beef dinner: consommé → main beef preparation → cheese course

    Weeks 17–18: Lamb & Game (NEW BLOCK — Proposal A)

    Session 1 — Lamb Fundamentals: Cuts and Flavor Profile Grilled Lamb Chops with Herb Crust + Tzatziki - Protein: Loin or rib lamb chops, direct-grilled over high heat - Sauce: Herb-and-garlic crust, served with homemade tzatziki - Starch: Grilled flatbread - Veg: Grilled zucchini with lemon

    Session 5 — PROJECT: Leg of Lamb Butterflied Leg of Lamb (Grilled) or Slow-Roasted Bone-In - Protein: Learner's choice of method — butterflied and marinated (mojo or herb) or slow-roasted bone-in - Starch: Arroz blanco - Salad: Fattoush with pomegranate

    Session 10 — PROJECT: The Lamb and Duck Dinner Party Three-course dinner: lamb soup → lamb main → duck dessert (duck fat caramel) - First: Lamb broth with orzo and herbs (from trimmings) - Main: Rack of lamb persillade (review Session 4) - Dessert: Tarte Tatin with duck fat pastry (demonstrates fat substitution)

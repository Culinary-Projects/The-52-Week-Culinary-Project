    Kitchen Equipment Guide

    For students starting out: This guide lists every piece of equipment used in the 52-week program, when you'll first need it, and how essential it is. Use this to plan your purchases in advance rather than scrambling week-of.

    Three tiers: - 🔴 Essential — You cannot complete the skill without this. Buy before the week shown. - 🟡 Recommended — You'll use it repeatedly and improvisation is awkward. Worth buying. - 🟢 Optional — Nice to have. A workaround exists and is noted.

    How to Use This Guide

    1. Before you start: Buy everything in the Essential column for Weeks 1–8. That list is short and cheap.
    2. Before each module: Check the module's equipment table and buy any new Essential items at least a week ahead.
    3. Specialty items: Japanese knives, bannetons, tamagoyaki pans — buy when the week arrives, not sooner. No point cluttering the counter.

    Notes on Quality

    • Knife: One great knife beats a block of mediocre ones. If you're buying new, the Victorinox Fibrox 8-inch gives professional results at home-cook prices. Japanese gyuto knives offer better edge retention but require more maintenance.
    • Cast iron: Lodge skillet and Dutch oven are the standard; pre-seasoned and durable for decades.
    • Thermometer: ThermoWorks Thermapen or ThermoPop are the reliable standards. Harbor Freight / cheap probes read slow and drift.
    • Scale: OXO Good Grips, Escali, or My Weigh KD-8000 all hit the 0.1g sweet spot.
    • Dutch oven: Le Creuset and Staub are lifetime purchases. Lodge enamel is the budget-friendly alternative.

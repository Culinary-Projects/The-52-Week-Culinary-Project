    The 52-Week Culinary Project — Full Program Overview

    Every block, every session, every meal — with components at a glance.

    Module 1: Foundational Skills

    Weeks 1–2: Knife Skills

    Session 1 — The Pinch Grip
    Pasta with Butter and Parmesan
    - Main: Parmesan-crusted pasta - Veg: Wilted baby spinach, sautéed in the pasta pan with olive oil, garlic, and lemon

    Session 2 — The Claw
    The Best Chicken Noodle Soup
    - Protein: Chicken breast — poach in the broth (or sous vide if you have one), then dice - Veg: Onion (charred, per recipe) into the stock; your diced carrot and celery into the soup for last 10 minutes - Starch: Egg noodles, cooked seperately

    Session 3 — Rock Chop vs. Push Cut
    Shrimp Scampi
    - Protein: Shrimp, sautéed in garlic butter and white wine - Veg/Salad: Arugula, Fennel & Orange Salad - Starch: Crusty bread or linguine

    Session 4 — Mise en Place
    Stir-Fried Beef and Broccoli
    - Protein: Thinly sliced flank steak, seared in very small batches - Veg: Broccoli florets, seared in the same pan - Starch: Steamed jasmine rice, started before you begin the stir-fry

    Session 5 — PROJECT MEAL: French Onion Soup
    French Onion Soup (Soupe à l'Oignon)

    Session 6 — The Julienne
    The Best Pad Thai
    - Protein: Shrimp, chicken, or tofu - Veg: Your julienned carrots and scallions, plus bean sprouts; finish with fresh basil chiffonade - Starch: Rice noodles, soaked per the recipe

    Session 7 — The Dice (Small, Medium, Large)
    Home Fries + Pan-Seared Crispy Chicken Thighs
    - Protein: Crispy pan-seared bone-in chicken thighs - Veg/Salad: Classic Green Salad with Vinaigrette - Starch: Home fries (your dice drill is the recipe)

    Session 8 — Breaking Down a Whole Chicken
    Chicken Piccata (use the breasts) + Burst Cherry Tomatoes
    - Protein: Chicken piccata using the breasts you broke down - Veg: Burst Cherry Tomatoes with Garlic and Olive Oil - Starch: Linguine — toss with the burst tomatoes and a splash of pasta water

    Session 9 — Speed Benchmark
    Shakshuka + leftover chicken from earlier in the block thighs (warmed in a skillet)
    - Protein: Eggs poached in the shakshuka sauce + leftover chicken from earlier in the block thighs - Veg: Onion, bell pepper, tomatoes (the shakshuka base) - Starch: Crusty bread for dipping

    Session 10 — PROJECT MEAL: Provençal Vegetable Tian
    Provençal Summer Vegetable Tian + crusty bread
    - Protein: Optional — serve with good burrata or a soft-boiled egg on top - Veg: The tian (eggplant, zucchini, squash, tomato) - Starch: Crusty baguette

    Weeks 3–4: Heat And Cooking

    Session 1 — Sauté: Hot Pan, Don't Crowd
    Sautéed Chicken Breasts with Quick Pan Sauce + Sautéed Zucchini with Garlic and Herbs + Steamed Rice
    - Protein: Sautéed boneless chicken breast, finished with a quick pan sauce of shallot, white wine, and butter - Veg: Sautéed zucchini — notice how a hot pan gives you color on the outside while keeping the inside tender - Starch: Steamed jasmine rice

    Session 2 — Roasting: Hot Oven, Dry Surface
    Sheet Pan Chicken Thighs with Roasted Vegetables + Simple Green Salad
    - Protein: Bone-in skin-on chicken thighs, roasted until crackling - Veg: Whatever is on the sheet pan — broccoli, potatoes, onions - Salad: Simple green salad alongside

    Session 3 — Braising: Low, Slow, Wet
    Braised Chicken Thighs with Tomatoes and Capers + Crusty bread or polenta
    - Protein: Braised chicken thighs in a rich tomato-caper sauce - Starch: Crusty bread to mop the sauce, or polenta

    Session 4 — Steaming and Poaching: Gentle, Wet Heat
    Perfect Poached Salmon + Steamed Bok Choy with Garlic Sauce + Steamed rice
    - Protein: Gently poached salmon — silky, delicate, nothing like a seared fillet - Veg: Steamed bok choy, dressed simply with garlic and sesame - Starch: Steamed rice

    Session 5 — PROJECT MEAL: Spatchcocked Roast Chicken
    Spatchcocked Roast Chicken + Roasted Potatoes + Simple Green Salad with Vinaigrette

    Session 6 — Searing Steak: The Art of Doing Nothing
    Perfect Pan-Seared Steak with Butter Basting + Creamy Mashed Potatoes + Simple Sautéed Green Beans
    - Protein: Ribeye or NY strip, pan-seared in cast iron, basted in butter, thyme, and garlic - Starch: Ultra-creamy mashed potatoes (use more butter than you're comfortable with) - Veg: Green beans sautéed in the steak pan drippings

    Session 7 — High-Heat Fish: Crispy Skin Without Fear
    Crispy Pan-Seared Salmon + Roasted Asparagus + Simple Arugula and Fennel Salad
    - Protein: Crispy-skinned salmon fillet, finished with lemon and capers - Veg: Roasted asparagus (high-heat oven, single layer, very dry) - Salad: Arugula salad alongside

    Session 8 — Broiling: Overhead Heat
    Broiled Salmon with Miso Glaze + Sautéed Bok Choy + Steamed rice
    - Protein: Miso-glazed salmon under the broiler — 8 minutes, no flipping needed - Veg: Sautéed or steamed bok choy - Starch: Steamed rice

    Session 9 — Recap Cook: No Recipes
    Braised Short Ribs + Creamy Polenta + Roasted Root Vegetables
    - Protein: Bone-in beef short ribs braised in red wine and beef stock until they collapse - Starch: Creamy parmesan polenta (the perfect landing pad for the braising liquid) - Veg: Roasted root vegetables (carrots, parsnips, turnips)

    Weeks 5–6: Mother Sauces

    Session 1 — Béchamel: The White Sauce
    Baked Mac and Cheese (Béchamel-Based) + Simple Green Salad
    - Main: Baked mac and cheese (the whole dish) - Starch: Pasta (built-in) - Salad: Simple green salad to cut through the richness

    Session 2 — Velouté: Stock Instead of Milk
    Chicken Pot Pie with Velouté Base + Simple green salad
    - Protein: Chicken (cooked and pulled into the filling) - Starch: The pastry crust - Veg: Peas, carrots, and onion in the filling - Salad: Simple green salad alongside - Protein: Ground beef and pork, slow-cooked into the sauce - Starch: Fresh tagliatelle or good dried pasta - Veg: Mirepoix (in the sauce) + Classic Caesar Salad alongside

    Session 4 — Espagnole and Demi-Glace (Simplified)
    Steak with Red Wine Pan Sauce + Creamy Mashed Potatoes + Roasted Broccolini
    - Protein: Pan-seared steak with a red wine reduction pan sauce - Starch: Ultra-creamy mashed potatoes - Veg: Roasted broccolini - Protein: Ground lamb or beef in the tomato layer - Sauce: Béchamel on top, baked to a golden crust - Veg: Roasted eggplant and potato layers - Salad: Greek salad alongside

    Session 6 — Hollandaise: The Emulsion
    Eggs Benedict with Hollandaise — yes, breakfast for dinner. It's legitimate when it teaches you hollandaise.
    - Protein: Canadian bacon, poached eggs - Sauce: Hollandaise (your skill for tonight) - Starch: English muffins, toasted - Veg/Salad: Simple Watercress Salad alongside

    Session 7 — Mayonnaise: Emulsion Without Heat
    Seafood Rolls with Homemade Mayo + Classic Coleslaw
    - Protein: Shrimp, scallop, or lobster roll - Veg: Coleslaw made with your homemade mayo - Starch: Brioche or potato rolls

    Session 8 — The Vinaigrette: A Temporary Emulsion
    Pan-Seared Salmon Niçoise Salad — a full composed dinner salad, not a side.
    - Protein: Seared salmon or tuna - Veg: Green beans, olives, tomatoes, hard-boiled eggs - Starch: Boiled potatoes (dressed warm with the vinaigrette — they absorb it better)

    Session 9 — Pan Sauce: The Working Sauce
    Pork Chops with Apple-Cider Pan Sauce + Roasted Sweet Potatoes + Simple Green Salad
    - Protein: Pan-seared pork chops with apple-cider pan sauce - Starch: Roasted sweet potatoes - Salad: Simple green salad with your from-scratch vinaigrette

    Session 10 — PROJECT MEAL: A Classic French Bistro Dinner (3 Courses)
    - Course: Dish - *Starter*: French Onion Dip with Homemade Potato Chips - *Main*: Steak Frites with Pan Sauce - *Salad*: Classic Lyonnaise Salad (Frisée aux Lardons)

    Weeks 7–8: Stocks And Eggs

    Session 0 — Soup Architecture (optional)
    Roasted Butternut Squash Soup + crusty bread + simple salad
    - Family: Technique - *Broth/consommé*: Clear liquid, flavored by solids - *Purée*: Cooked vegetables blended smooth; no cream needed - *Cream soup*: Purée + cream or béchamel for richness and texture - *Bisque*: Shellfish shells simmered into the base; always cream-finished - *Chowder*: Chunky; potato base; salt pork/bacon; cream-finished

    Session 1 — Chicken Stock from Scratch
    Quick Weeknight Ramen Using Homemade Stock
    - Broth: Your homemade chicken stock, seasoned with soy and mirin - Protein: Soft-boiled eggs, chashu pork or rotisserie chicken, scallions - Starch: Ramen noodles

    Session 2 — Pan Sauces from Real Stock
    Chicken with Rich Pan Sauce (using your homemade stock) + Roasted Potatoes + Bitter Greens Salad
    - Protein: Sautéed chicken with a pan sauce made from your Block 7 stock - Starch: Crispy roasted potatoes - Salad: Bitter greens salad with a sharp vinaigrette

    Session 3 — Fish Stock + Fumet
    Bouillabaisse — the classic Provençal fish stew, built on your fresh fish stock
    - Protein: Mixed seafood — shrimp, clams, white fish - Stock: Your fresh fish fumet - Starch: Grilled bread rubbed with garlic (rouille on the side) - Veg: Fennel, leek, tomato in the stew

    Session 4 — Vegetable Stock + Risotto
    Parmesan Risotto + Roasted Mushrooms + Simple Arugula Salad
    - Protein: Roasted mushrooms (the star) + parmesan as umami - Starch: Risotto (the main dish) - Salad: Arugula alongside to cut richness

    Session 5 — PROJECT MEAL: A Whole Soup Kitchen Day
    - Time: Task - Morning: Big batch beef/veal stock (6–8 hrs, mostly passive) - Dinner: French Onion Soup using your fresh beef stock - Sides: Simple Green Salad + good bread

    Session 6 — The French Scramble
    French Scrambled Eggs + Smoked Salmon with Crème Fraîche + Toasted Sourdough + Simple Green Salad
    - Protein: French scrambled eggs + smoked salmon - Starch: Toasted sourdough - Salad: Simple green salad

    Session 7 — The Perfect Poached Egg
    Poached Eggs Over Sautéed Greens with Crispy Bacon + Roasted Tomatoes + Toasted bread
    - Protein: Poached eggs + crispy bacon - Veg: Wilted greens (kale or spinach), roasted cherry tomatoes - Starch: Good toast

    Session 8 — The Omelette
    The French Omelette + Classic Green Salad + Crusty bread
    - Protein: Two omelettes (one per person or two attempts) - Filling: Gruyère, herbs, or mushrooms - Salad: Classic green salad with vinaigrette - Starch: Crusty bread

    Session 9 — Custard: Eggs as Thickener — Quiche Lorraine
    - Quiche Lorraine (lardons + Gruyère + chive)
    - Shell: Store-bought shortcrust — pastry dough is covered fully in Block 47 - Custard: 3 eggs + ¾ cup heavy cream + salt + white pepper - Cheese: Gruyère grated fresh; pre-shredded bags have coatings that prevent clean melting - Bake: 325°F, 30–40 min; pull when center still has a gentle wobble

    Session 10 — PROJECT MEAL: A Complete Egg Dinner Party (3 Courses)
    - Course: Dish - *Starter*: Deviled Eggs (vehicle for your homemade mayo from Block 6) - *Main*: Shakshuka (scaled up, served family-style with salad and bread) - *Dessert*: Crème Brûlée (classic baked egg custard, finished with a torch) - Block 1–2 time: Today's time - _____ min: _____ min

    Weeks 11–12: Vegetables Plant Forward

    Session 0 — Two Purées, One Method (optional)
    Celery root and parsnip purées as the base + seared duck breast or seared scallops on top + a simple green salad
    - Purée: Method notes - *Celery root (celeriac)*: Sweat in butter, add stock, simmer until completely soft, blend very fine, strain - *Parsnip with roasted garlic*: Roast garlic until golden; simmer parsnips in stock; blend with roasted garlic and butter

    Session 1 — Blanching, Shocking, and Color Science
    Blanched and shocked asparagus + haricots verts + broccolini — dressed with brown butter and toasted almonds, served at room temperature
    - Pigment: Vegetable examples - *Chlorophyll* (green): Broccoli, asparagus, spinach, peas - *Anthocyanin* (red/purple): Red cabbage, purple carrots, beets - *Carotenoid* (orange/yellow): Carrots, squash, sweet potato - *Betanin* (deep red): Beets - Vegetables: Asparagus, haricots verts, broccolini — all blanched and shocked - Dressing: Brown butter + toasted almonds + lemon zest + fleur de sel - Protein (optional): Soft-boiled eggs on top to make it a dinner

    Session 2 — Roasting: Caramelization and Concentration
    Sheet Pan Dinner — roasted carrots, cauliflower, and beets with whatever protein you prefer + a tahini dressing or romesco
    - Vegetables: Carrots (halved), cauliflower (florets), golden beets (quartered) - Dressing: Tahini sauce or romesco - Protein: Chicken thighs on the same pan, or eat vegetarian

    Session 3 — Sauté, Braise, and the Crucifer Redemption
    - Crispy Brussels Sprouts with Fish Sauce and Crispy Shallots — this dish converts Brussels sprout haters
    - Brussels sprouts: Halved, pressed cut-side into very hot oil; don't move; then toss - Cabbage: Wedges, cast iron, very high heat; miso glaze brushed at the end

    Session 4 — Grilling and Charring: Fire as Flavor
    A grilled and charred vegetable spread:
    - Vegetable: Method - Eggplant: Full char over gas flame or under broiler; peel and season - Zucchini: Direct grill, slight char marks, finish with lemon + mint - Scallions: Whole on the grill until charred and collapsed - Peppers: Full char, steam in bag, peel and seed

    Session 5 — PROJECT: Plant-Forward Dinner Party
    - Course: Dish - *Starter*: Roasted beet salad with whipped feta and pistachios - *Main*: Seared mushroom "steaks" + celery root purée + pan sauce from mushroom fond - *Side*: Blanched and shocked green beans with brown butter - *Bread*: Good sourdough

    Session 0 — Quick Pickles & First Ferment (optional)
    Use the pickles tonight on whatever you're eating — tacos, a grain bowl, grilled fish. Quick pickles don't need a dedicated meal. They improve everything they touch.

    Session 6 — The Composed Salad: Dressing Is a Technique
    Caesar salad (full portion, not a side) + half a roast chicken or grilled fish on top + crusty bread
    - Dressing: Raw egg yolk + anchovy + garlic + lemon + Worcestershire; emulsify with oil - Croutons: Day-old bread, torn not cut; tossed in olive oil; bake until very crispy - Lettuce: Romaine; the spine of the leaf stays crispy even under dressing

    Session 7 — Legumes and Beans: The Forgotten Protein
    Braised white beans on toast + crispy chickpeas + wilted greens + poached egg on top
    - White beans: Braised in stock with garlic, rosemary, anchovy - Chickpeas: Crispy-fried in cast iron, paprika + lemon - Greens: Wilted spinach or kale in the bean liquid - Egg (optional): Poached on top to make it a substantial dinner

    Session 8 — Mushrooms: Umami in Plant Form
    Mushroom Bourguignon — the vegetarian version of the French braise, using a mix of cremini, shiitake, and oyster mushrooms + a proper wine reduction
    - Mushrooms: Mix of varieties; seared separately, then combined - Sauce: Red wine + stock + tomato paste + herbs and aromatics - Base: Creamy mashed potatoes, polenta, or buttered egg noodles

    Session 9 — A Completely Vegetable-Driven Main
    Stuffed roasted peppers (3 colors — red, yellow, orange) + simple tomato sauce beneath them + salad
    - Peppers: Halved, par-roasted before stuffing to soften walls - Stuffing: Sautéed onion + garlic + cooked farro or rice + cherry tomatoes + parmesan - Sauce beneath: Simple tomato concassé or straight from your Week 5 sauce skills

    Session 10 — PROJECT: The Vegetable Tasting Menu
    - Course: Dish - 1: Celery root purée with roasted garlic oil - 2: Blanched asparagus with brown butter and fried capers - 3: Charred cabbage with miso-sesame - 4: Mushroom bourguignon on toast - 5: Quick-pickled vegetables + bread

    Weeks 17–18: Beef Fundamentals

    Session 0 — Beef Fabrication (optional)
    The short ribs you trim tonight — braise them tomorrow or this weekend (see Week 16 for the full recipe). Tonight pair with whatever you have: bread and a salad is fine.
    - Primal: Characteristics - *Chuck* (shoulder): Well-worked muscle, lots of collagen - *Rib*: Less-worked, heavily marbled - *Short loin*: Tender, central back - *Sirloin*: Moderately worked - *Round* (rear leg): Lean, tough - *Brisket* (chest): Very tough, collagen-heavy - *Flank/plate*: Flat, thin, fibrous - *Shank*: Very tough, maximum gelatin

    Session 1 — The Dry Brine + Reverse Sear Method
    Reverse-seared ribeye + Serious Eats — Rich and Creamy Mashed Potatoes + simple green salad
